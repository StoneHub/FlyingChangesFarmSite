Aviation roots, farm future.

Flying Changes Farm spans both sides of Pumpkintown Road in Marietta, South Carolina. The property carries a multi-generational aviation legacy while supporting horses, goats, campers, and a growing list of software projects.

Family aviation legacy

The property has always pointed skyward. Family pilots have flown the Carolina foothills for decades, and the hangar keeps that tradition alive with Pinnacle Paramotor training and maintenance.

  • Runway carved from family pastureland.
  • Paramotor operations integrated with herd movement.
  • Hangar doubles as classroom, lounge, and code lab.
  • Flight culture informs how guests and builders move through the farm.

Blending ag and technology

The farm balances animal care, primitive camping, and software development. Horses and goats dictate the daily rhythm; the workshop captures those needs in tools like PickMyRoute and Care Calendar.

  • Rotational grazing data feeds into mapping prototypes.
  • Campground logistics inspire booking and notification systems.
  • Hangar workspace hosts code sprints between flights.
  • Community visitors test early software builds on site.

Hurricane Helene recovery

The farm is still rebuilding after Hurricane Helene. Fence lines, drainage, and hangar infrastructure are mid-upgrade, and every campsite stay or software launch ties into that recovery work.

  • Ongoing repairs to fencing on both sides of Pumpkintown Road.
  • Hangar roof checks and runoff improvements underway.
  • Storm data informs future GIS and alert projects.
  • Guests receive pre-arrival updates on active work zones.

Future goals

  • Launch Hipcamp listings once rebuild milestones are met.
  • Expand the hangar lounge for pilots, campers, and coworking.
  • Ship public versions of PickMyRoute and Care Calendar.
  • Archive family aviation stories alongside new software releases.
