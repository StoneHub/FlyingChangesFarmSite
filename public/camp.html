Primitive stays on Pumpkintown Road.

Campers tuck into the pasture-side clearing at Flying Changes Farm in Marietta, South Carolina. The site is primitive by design, supported by the hangar’s bathroom, lounge, and storm shelter. Email while we finalize the public Hipcamp listing.

Included comforts

  • Pasture-side clearing with picnic table and fire ring.
  • Hangar lounge with bathroom, kitchenette, and workspace.
  • Power outlets available inside the hangar for charging gear.
  • Access to runoff water for cleanup (bring drinking water).

RVs & vehicles

  • RVs and trailers may park on-site; no direct hookups in the clearing.
  • Shore power is available at the hangar for limited use.
  • Gravel drive supports standard vans and mid-size rigs.
  • Please coordinate arrival so we can guide backing and turnaround.

Bring along

  • Drinking water, coolers, and food.
  • Camp chairs and lanterns or headlamps.
  • Rain plan for tents—hangar lounge stays open during storms.
  • Respect for the animals and runway operations.

After Hurricane Helene

We are rebuilding fence lines and improving drainage following Hurricane Helene. Temporary structures protect campers while permanent upgrades are underway.

  • Expect fresh gravel and ongoing fence work during visits.
  • Hangar remains a safe hub for storms.
  • Debris piles are staged away from the campsite.
  • Email updates go out before each stay with the latest progress.
